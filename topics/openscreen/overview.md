# OpenScreen

## What It Is

OpenScreen is a free, open-source desktop screen recording tool built on Electron with a modern web-tech stack (React, TypeScript, PixiJS). It provides basic recording, editing, and export capabilities without subscriptions or watermarks, positioning itself as a cost-conscious alternative to Screen Studio ($89/year). Currently in beta (v1.3.0, April 2, 2026) with MIT licensing enabling both personal and commercial use.

## Key Concepts

- **Free and open-source:** No licensing costs, no watermarks, MIT license permits commercial derivatives
- **Core features:** Screen/window recording, manual zoom (6 customizable levels), timeline editing, effects, MP4/WebM export up to 4K/60fps
- **Manual editing:** Zoom points must be added post-recording; no automatic cursor-following or transcript generation
- **Beta stability:** Acknowledged as "very much in beta and might be buggy"; v1.3.0 introduced regressions (startup failures, UI bugs)
- **Market position:** Occupies the "free, open-source, uncomplicated" tier alongside Cap and Kap; differentiated from OBS (streaming), Loom (quick sharing), Screen Studio (polished automation)

## Context

- **Developers and enthusiasts:** Strong appeal to cost-conscious solo creators and technical users who value control and transparency
- **When to consider:** Simple demos, developer showcases, internal documentation where manual editing burden is acceptable
- **When to avoid:** Business workflows requiring transcript, audio cleanup, automated polish, or rapid iteration cycles
- **Market trend:** 2026 screen recording market trending toward AI-powered automation (auto-zoom, auto-captions, auto-editing) as baseline; OpenScreen positioned as basic alternative, not competitive replacement
